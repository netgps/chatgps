ChatGPS Report

Abstract

This application aims to address the problem of phone users' thought dispersion, the cause of this dispersion is mainly related to the excessive use of a large number of applications daily, so this application offers an integrated model of built-in use of the most important technologies needed by users, as the application contains GPS location tracking, accompanied by the weather, driver assistant, chat, accessing and controlling devices.

Introduction

A vast category of users suffer from the problems of mismanaging their personal activities. As a result, these people lose the happiness. This lose is due to several reasons, the most likely is because they find themselves facing the desires of others, yes, those desires that they did not establish their lives for it, which makes them feel like they have nothing in their lives. Therefore, it was necessary to create some applications that give users the ability to control everything in their lives without negatively affect on others. That is, increasing the compatibility ratio between users. In this regard, we have created an application that enables users to control their phones remotely, track many contacts' locations as well as communicate with them at the same time and repair contacts' devices using shell (if you have the authorisation). Also, this application makes it possible to know the shortest routes, bus and train stations, as well as calculate the real distance between various places and contacts. Unlike other applications, this one does not store users' data on any online database, just saves the data in the device, this data is encrypted with a custom algorithm (for each user). This makes it impossible to access it by others, let alone read it. Therefore, servers only make sure that the account is registered and connected with contacts. In the other hand, we created a correction system, that can fix almost probably errors appear when add, change contacts or formatting your device, so the app can restore the history of your contacts without any online database. Also, all data are encrypted by the sender's application using several algorithms, and decrypted by the receiver's application. To eliminate the possibility of unethical use, blockout features have been added, as well as reporting any abusive, blackmailing or obscene speech; any user can activate it, which will allow it to be sent to developers via the server. For this application, more than forty servers have been used that can be increased if we need more, working 24/7 in a week, to ensure the convenience of users. It should also be noted that the application is free, and does not include any annoying ads, currently, this application consists of 3 levels out of 8, the rest will be shared if the application gets the users' interest. The first level is for people interested only in GPS, distances, areas, weather, and trace routes. So, they wouldn't have to create an account. The second level begins with making a new account for your device so that if the GPS does not turn on the device, the application will take a random location close to the destination where the device is located. While all contacts can be added via sending invitations. Once an invitation is accepted, both users will be able to know each other's locations, and then start the conversation, the GPS usage of the other user's device can appear as a red flag (if the GPS is not activated). The third level begins when the user has the authorisation to access a first contact device. First The user must be an adult, then, he must send the shell request, after accepting this request by the contact, the user can access the other device shell, and then, he can easily move, create and delete files/directories, also, he can getting files from the other device if they have the same full name.

Architecture

I- Scientific GPS (SciGPS)

The level of SciGPS is the basic level in this application, at this level the user does not need to create any account or share something with contacts, it is open for all public categories, this level contains the traditional features available in most GPS-MAPS applications. Therefore, this level is the basic platform that has been used to create the highest levels with a good quality and experience.

1- Maps

The map is one of the most important basic feature. This map will download and store automatically while searching for places. The application currently includes 7 different OpenStreetMap, including patterns that specialize in knowing the locations of bus and train stations and the different roads styles, including public organisations and private facilities, others maps for localising green spaces, and those to know the heights relative to the level of the sea. Therefore, the multiplicity of choices is primarily in favour of users. The interface includes the status for the latitude and longitude, and zooms below the map, which helps the user to know exact the locations, when the user clicks on the coordinate status, the currently available maps menu appears so that the user can change the map style automatically.

Under this status, there is a button for the map centre coordinates and the zoom value while moving the map. Then at the bottom, there are three buttons, the 'select state' button can change the nature of the stats from 1D to 2D or vice versa, then the 'Find Path' button to activate the 'Trace Route' feature, will only work by adding at least two markers. Then on the right we find the GPS button, to locate the device on the map, it will not be activated unless there is internet. Figure 1.b shows the list of maps integrated into the application, which can be shown by pressing the coordinate button and zoom. The 'Institutions' style (Figure 1.c) shows and recognizes all institutions' locations in each street, the Roadways pattern (Figure 1.d) shows the main roads in the city, then the landscape pattern shows the most important green areas in the wilderness valleys, then the transport pattern shows the railways distribution, train and bus stations, then the highlands pattern shows the elevations between plains, plateaus and mountains. Thus, the most important maps have been included to help the most active social groups and motivate them to do their best to build a good future for the next generations.

2- Search

The location search feature is one of the most important components of applications that are based on maps, as the search feature makes it easier for users to find places that are difficult to reach by only browsing the map. Therefore, it has been included in our application, it is enough to click on the magnify button at the map top, then enter one or more words indicating the place that the user wants to reach, while typing the words, addresses appear indicating the location of the written word including its coordinates. After pressing this address, the map automatically moves to the centre of the indicated place, if the place does not match the user's desires, he can re-search again and again without any difficulties.

Figure 2.a indicates the status of the application when it runs, then after pressing the magnify button, a place appears to enter the search words (Figure 2.b), at the beginning of entering the words, the address automatically appears to match the listed words (Figure 2.c), after pressing address, the map moves to the indicated place (Figure 2.d), you can research for different places using the word and move to it immediately with zoom = 18. 3- Distance

Calculating geographical distances is a feature available in some maps via the Internet, and it is also very useful in GPS applications, it can be used to find out the distance between two remote locations, or between the user's location and the place he wants to go, which will help him estimate the time required for this mobility, depending on the means of transportation that he can use and the type of roads he encounters during his movement, Thus, by knowing the distance, users can manage their time smoothly.

When the application is running, and according to Figure 3.a, the user presses the select state button, then a menu appears including the distance and area states (or can deny by choosing select state). When the user clicks on the distance label, the first marker appears in the centre of the map (Figure 3.b). It can be moved to any location that the user wants to make as a starting point, for example, it has been converted to the French country, after that, the user can add a new marker by clicking on the latest marker, then 'Add New Location' to display the second marker (Figure 3.d-e). Moving one marker to another place, the value of the geographical distance between them appears at the bottom of the screen in meters (Figure 3.f). To change the distance unit, it is enough to click on the distance value to display all the units integrated into the application, and then the user can choose one of them, in this regard, most users like to use large units to measure large distances and small units to measure small distances. Some want to use small units to measure all distances. For example, here in Figure 3.h, inch was chosen instead of meter, which increased the accuracy of the distance from 10 digits to 12 digits. To add more than two markers, it is enough to click on the latest marker, and then preview the distance across the screen so that the distance value is the sum of the distance between all markers indicated by the blue line (Figure 3.j). Users can add infinity of markers in the same way (Figure 3.k), and zooming in or out of the map does not make any distortions in the lines or markers locations (Figure 3.l). Users can remove some markers by pressing the last one, then pressing the 'remove' label (Figure 3.m), or pressing the distance button and then the 'select state' label, and then all markers disappear from the map (Figure 3.n-o).

5- GPS

GPS is the most important element in tracking applications and knowing the locations of contacts without the need to call them to find out their locations, so these applications must contain a high degree of accuracy and simplicity.

To determine the GPS location of the device, first, the device must have a GPS service, while all smartphones have GPS, but some other smart devices don't, such as laptops and many types of cars and trucks, for those, users can sell GPS adapters and connect them with the control panel, which can enable applications to found the device location on the map, so the smartphone was used instead of laptop in Figure 5 to clarify the mechanism of the application's handling of GPS, in some smartphones GPS, CAMERA, MICROPHONE services do not work with new applications until after allowing them through the settings in the permissions section, so if the app can't turn on GPS, CAMERA, ... etc, please make sure that the device given all the permissions needed through the settings. Well, after the application is running, please click on the Run GPS button at the bottom of the App (Figure 5.a), then make sure that the Internet and GPS services (Figure 5.b) are turned on, just by moving the map your location will automatically appear on the map (Figure 5.c). To activate zoom to the location, it is enough to click on the icon in the map, then 'Zoom To Location' (Figure 5.d), then the map will move to the GPS location with zoom=18 (Figure 5.e), then the user can move the map until the images appear in higher quality (Figure 5.f-g), then to stop GPS, it is enough to press 'Stop GPS' in green to automatically disappear the location from the map (Figure 5.h).

6- Weather

The navigation system is essential in track or travel applications to know the characteristics of the weather at any place in the world, which helps users to prepare their day program with better accuracy, statistics of the characteristics of the current weather have been added for the location in the middle of the map. So that the temperature on the right can be observed in °C, then the ratio of humidity, the value of air pressure, then the wind speed in meters per second accompanied by its direction according to the clockwise (Figure 6). For example, in the Desert of Africa (Figure 6.a), we find that during the evening on the fourth of June 2024, the temperature is 38 °C and the humidity is 9%, meaning that the weather is dry and the pressure is low 1008 Pa, which means a lack of oxygen in this place, and the wind speed is 6.6m/s, equivalent to 29 km/h towards sunset. Our second example is a location in the middle of the Mediterranean Sea during the evening, where the temperature is 22°C, which is a moderate temperature, but the humidity is high (79%) and the pressure is 1015 Pa, and the wind speed is 4.79 m/s, which is approximately 17 km / h towards the west, meaning that the atmosphere is relatively stable (Figure 6.b), then Figure 6.c shows a temperature of 26°C, a humidity of approximately 65%, and a moderate wind speed of about 18km/h, on the shore of the central Mediterranean island, these statistics are same with Figure 6.b statistics, then we also have Figure 6.d which shows the weather status in the north at the afternoon, so that the temperature is approximately 0°C and high humidity 98% and pressure of 1018Pa, all this due to the high oxygen density, and the winds are semi-stable by about 11 km/h towards the southeast. Figure 6.e represents weather status in the islands of the central Atlantic with moderate temperature, high humidity and pressure, as expected, with weak winds, then in the west of the USA in Figure 6.f, we notice a low temperature with moderate humidity as well as moderate pressure, but the wind speed is high due to the high intensity of the region that faces at the foot of the mountains (38 km/h) towards the southwest. In Figure 6.g we observe a decrease in the temperature in the capital of Australia during the night with high humidity (95%) and stable winds, which makes sense during the month of June. As well as Figure 6.h, the status is logical in the islands of the central Pacific Ocean, with moderate temperature and high humidity, but at low pressure, which may raise several questions about the weather of these islands with high humidity low pressure, which indicates an imbalance in the density of the region's gases, which may be due to the high percentage of oxygen compared to other gases.
